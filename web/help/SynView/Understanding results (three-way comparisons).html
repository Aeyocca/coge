How do I interpret a three way comparison?
 
Understanding the results of a three way comparison is no different than a two way comparison, except that there are three pair-wise comparisons to consider instead of only one.  In the above example, there are three genomic regions, the bottom two have gene models and the top one is unannotated sequence.  Just as with the two-way comparison, blast HSPs are drawn as colored boxes where each color and number indicates which the pair of regions that make an HSP.  Since we have three sequences, there are three pair-wise bl2seq comparisons to generate. (seq1<->seq2, seq2<->seq3, seq1<->seq3)  And each pair-wise comparison gets its own color for its HSPs.  So, in the above example, HSPs generated from Region1 vs. Region2 are colored blue, Regions2 vs. Region3 are red, and Region1 vs. Region3 are green.
 
Now, when we look at the above example, we can see some very interesting patterns.  The first is that even though the top genomic region (region 1) is unannotated, using sequence similarity to the other two regions gives us an indication of the gene content of the region. The second is that there is synteny between the three regions (well, as best as you can tell with two genes per region).  And finally, when you look at the left-most gene in the bottom two genomic regions (highlighted in yellow), there is a discrepancy in the gene annotation.  Region 2's model is longer than Region 3's and has an annotated mRNA that extends beyond the coding region (which is an indication that there is experimental support, such as EST or cDNA data, for this transcript).  Given Region 3's lack of a transcript model for this gene and the obvious additional conserved sequence with Region 2, Region 3's gene is probably a mis-annotation.  However, it is important to note that there is only minor sequence similarity to the top chromosomal region which may indicate 3' end of these gene is evolving rapidly (or has been rendered non-functional).  This is valuable information to have for anyone interested in researching this gene in Region 3.
